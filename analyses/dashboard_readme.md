# Global Development Project dashboard (In Progress)

### Dashboard Overview
This dashboard explores two key development indicators and one demographic indicator for the world’s twenty most populous countries between 2020 and 2024. These are: 
•	GDP per capita
•	Life expectancy
•	Population levels
This dashboard provides a clear, visual summary of how economic performance, life expectancies and population levels have changed over the last five years. 
This dashboard has been developed to answer the project’s key analytical questions:
•	Which countries have seen the biggest rise in GDP per capita, life expectancy, and population over the last five years?
•	How does population growth compare across the world’s 20 most populous countries?
•	Is there a relationship between GDP per capita and life expectancy?
•	Which countries have the fastest declining populations?
•	Which countries have the fastest growing populations?

### Purpose
This dashboard uses the final curated marts tables to visually explore development and demographic trends across the world’s 20 most populous countries.
This dashboard highlights countries that have experienced the most significant changes in:
•	GDP per capita
•	Life expectancy
•	Population levels
The analysis is limited to the 20 most populous countries, providing a focused yet globally representative view of development trends.

### Key insights
GDP per capita growth (2020–2024)
The strongest increases in GDP per capita were observed in these countries:
•	Türkiye (+80.6%)
•	Mexico (+60.5%)
•	Russian Federation (+47.3%)
Countries with notable declines in GDP per capita include:
•	Nigeria (–61.2%)
•	Japan (–18.8%)
All other countries experienced positive GDP per capita growth.

Life expectancy trends
Countries showing the largest improvements in life expectancy:
•	Mexico (+7.1%)
•	Bangladesh (+5.6%)
•	Nigeria (+3.8%)
Japan is the only country with a slight decline:
•	Japan (–1.2%)
China, Philippines, Türkiye, Thailand, and Tanzania maintained the same life expectancy in 2024 as in 2020. All other countries saw modest improvements.

Population growth
Countries with the fastest growing populations are the following:
•	Tanzania (+12.4%)
•	Ethiopia (+11.1%)
•	Nigeria (+8.7%)
Countries with declining populations include:
•	Japan (–1.8%)
•	Russian Federation (–1.2%)
•	China (–0.2%)
Relationship Between GDP per Capita and Life Expectancy
The scatter plots for 2020 and 2024 show a clear positive correlation:
Countries with higher GDP per capita generally tend to have a higher life expectancy.
This relationship is consistent across both years and is clearly illustrated in the dashboard’s comparative scatter plots.
